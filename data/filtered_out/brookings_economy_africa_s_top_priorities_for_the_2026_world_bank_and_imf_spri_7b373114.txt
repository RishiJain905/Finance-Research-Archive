April 8, 2026
The global economy is marked by increased debt vulnerabilities, fragmenting international trade, and impacts from artificial intelligence technologies. African countries are no less affected by these changes.
For African countries, success depends on maintaining macroeconomic stability, investing in human and physical capital, advancing structural reform, and taking advantage of the African Continental Free Trade Area.
A major challenge remains balancing immediate needs with long-term development goals. The World Bank is working on a Country Partnership Framework for the Sahel, as countries in the region share similar security and humanitarian challenges that have made long-term planning difficult.
Africa is full of promising cases, some examples being Kenya, which has reduced deficits and stabilized inflation, Côte d’Ivoire, which has maintained stable infrastructure-led growth, and Zambia, which has successfully restructured its debt. Senegal and Cabo Verde have also shown the power of job creation for economic transformation.
